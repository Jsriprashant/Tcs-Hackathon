
# Supernova Motors – General Services Agreement (GSA)

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Client:** [Dummy Client Corp., 98 Crescent Blvd., Austin, TX]  
**Supernova Motors, Inc.** (“Supernova”), a global EV, hybrid, ADAS, and mobility‑systems manufacturer headquartered in San Jose, CA.

This Agreement governs engineering, autonomous‑driving software development, mobility‑platform integration, fleet optimization work, R&D support, and hardware‑software deployment that Supernova performs for Client.

## 1. Engagement

### 1.1 Incorporation of Engagement Agreements
Each Statement of Work (“SOW”) or Engagement Agreement incorporates this GSA and supersedes conflicting terms.

### 1.2 Performance of Services
Supernova will perform services using its own equipment, test labs, ADAS simulation suites, and engineering processes. Subcontractors (e.g., firmware specialists, battery chemists, mobility‑analytics engineers) may be engaged as needed.

### 1.3 Change Requests
Client may submit modifications via written change request. Cost, schedule, and service‑impact evaluations will be provided before approval.

### 1.4 Competitive Work
Supernova may work with other OEMs, mobility providers, or EV/ADAS firms unless exclusivity is purchased separately.

### 1.5 Independent Contractor
Supernova operates independently, not as employee, partner, or agent of Client.

## 2. Compensation

### 2.1 Fees
Fees are defined in each SOW. Rates depend on engineering tier:
- ADAS & autonomy engineers: $240/hr  
- EV powertrain engineers: $220/hr  
- Cloud telematics developers: $200/hr  
- Mobility analytics specialists: $180/hr  

### 2.2 Delay Compensation
If Client causes delays (e.g., failure to provide vehicle access, data logs, testing facilities), Supernova may invoice up to **$700/day** of lost momentum.

## 3. Confidentiality
All proprietary EV schematics, ADAS models, ML datasets, telematics APIs, and customer‑fleet data are confidential.

## 4. Deliverables

### 4.1 Ownership
Upon full payment, Deliverables—including ADAS modules, simulation reports, firmware builds, mobility dashboards—transfer to Client. Pre‑existing Supernova IP is licensed, not sold.

### 4.2 Portfolio Use
Supernova may display non‑sensitive high‑level outcomes for marketing unless restricted by Client.

### 4.3 Acceptance
Client must notify deficiencies within 7 business days.

## 8. Termination
30‑day notice; immediate termination for material breach. Work‑to‑date must be paid.

## 9. General Provisions
Governing law: Delaware.  
Notices via certified mail or encrypted email.  
Prevailing party may recover legal fees.
